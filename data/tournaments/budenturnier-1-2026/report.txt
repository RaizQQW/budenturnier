BUDENTURNIER 1 2026 — EVENT REPORT
====================================
19 players | 5 Swiss rounds + Top 4 bracket | April 11, 2026


THE FORMAT
----------
A custom constructed format drawing from Innistrad (ISD), Shadows over Innistrad (SOI),
Midnight Hunt (MID), Crimson Vow (VOW), Strixhaven (STX), and M15 as a base set.
An extensive ban list shaped the meta heavily — and directly explains several of the
meta calls that did and didn't pan out.

Key bans relevant to pre-tournament predictions:

- Lightning Strike, Fiery Temper, Thermo-Alchemist, Blasphemous Act
  These four cards are the backbone of Mono Red Burn. Their bans didn't just weaken
  the archetype — they made it nonviable. The prediction of Mono Red showing up was
  reasonable from a raw power-level standpoint, but the format was deliberately
  designed to prevent it.

- Thalia's Lieutenant, Katilda Dawnhart Martyr, Voice of the Blessed,
  Geist of Saint Traft, Reckless Stormseeker
  Targeted cuts to keep Humans and aggressive two-drop synergy strategies below
  broken. Humans survived as a playable but not dominant archetype.

- Snapcaster Mage, Liliana of the Veil, Teferi Who Slows the Sunset,
  Bloodtithe Harvester, Meathook Massacre
  Capping the ceiling of tempo and control, preventing instant-speed graveyard
  recursion loops and one-sided sweepers.

The format rewarded midrange and tempo-forward decks that could still generate card
advantage without leaning on the most broken individual cards.

WHY LEI GAO WON: MARDU MIDRANGE
---------------------------------
Decklist core: Graveyard Trespasser x4, Sorin the Mirthless x3, Voldaren
Bloodcaster x2, Florian x2, Edgar x1, Anje x1, backed by Infernal Grasp,
Vanishing Verse, Fateful Absence, Humiliate, Duress.

Swiss record: 4W-0L-1D | Bracket: 3 matches, 4-0 in games (swept every bracket
series). Game win rate of 77.78% — highest at the event by a significant margin.

What made this deck dominant:

1. THE RIGHT THREATS FOR THE FORMAT
   Graveyard Trespasser is a 3/3 with ward that eats graveyards on attack. In a
   field with BG Graveyard (Luc), Abzan Control using Seasons Past recursion, and
   artifact synergies, this card was universally good. It attacks well, blocks well,
   and disrupts the two main value engines in the format.

2. SORIN THE MIRTHLESS GENERATES IMMEDIATE VALUE
   At 4 mana, Sorin either draws a card and puts it into play (a 4-mana cantrip
   threat), or makes a 1/1 flying lifelinker. He is never slow. Against control he
   provides a clock and card advantage simultaneously. Against aggro he gains life
   and makes chump blockers. In a format without Lightning Strike he survives
   burn-based removal.

3. SURGICAL DISRUPTION SUITE
   Duress + Humiliate as hand disruption meant Lei Gao could strip a sweeper
   (Vanquish the Horde) or a key threat (Seasons Past, Adeline) before it mattered.
   Humiliate's learn clause then tutored whatever answer he needed from the sideboard
   — extra Burn Down the House against tokens, End the Festivities against small
   humans, Fracture against Ensoul Artifact, Go Blank against control.

4. TEMPO WITHOUT BEING ALL-IN AGGRO
   Mardu wasn't trying to win on turn 4. It was grinding — efficient removal to
   neutralize early threats, then developing threats that required multiple answers.
   Florian rewards connecting in combat with free card selection. Bloodcaster makes
   Bats when you spend mana. The deck's threats all generated incremental value
   without dying to a single removal spell.

5. THE WHITE SPLASH WAS ESSENTIALLY FREE
   Isolated Chapel, Shattered Sanctum, Shineshadow Snarl gave access to Vanishing
   Verse (exile any non-land permanent for instant-speed mana — hits Adeline,
   Tireless Tracker, Intrepid Adversary) and Fateful Absence (hits planeswalkers
   and creatures).

The R1 draw with Julian (1-1) was the only Swiss blemish for both players. That draw
defined bracket seeding, and crucially the two players met again in the finals —
where Lei Gao had clearly identified the correct answers to the Abzan plan.


JULIAN'S RUN: ABZAN CONTROL
-----------------------------
Swiss: 4W-0L-1D (13 points, opponent win% of 64.00% — toughest schedule in the field)
Bracket: Won vs Fabian M 2-1 | Lost to Lei Gao 0-2 in Finals

WHAT WORKED:
The deck was correctly designed for the expected field. Professor of Symbology is a
2/1 Human Wizard that learns on ETB — fetching a Lesson from outside the game
(Environmental Sciences, Introduction to Prophecy, Mascot Exhibition), enabling a
flexible toolbox sideboard.

Sedgemoor Witch is a 3/2 with menace that generates a 1/1 Pest token (with
lifelink-on-death) whenever YOU cast your first instant or sorcery each turn. Each
removal spell — Flunk, Vanishing Verse, Humiliate — is simultaneously a
token-generator, building a side army while answering threats.

Vanquish the Horde as a scalable sweeper at 2WW (discounted by creatures in play)
was the correct anti-Humans finisher — hitting the board at instant speed for minimal
mana when facing 4-6 creatures.

Seasons Past recursion was the intended inevitability engine. In a long game,
recurring Flunk + Vanishing Verse + Vanquish creates a loop of card advantage and
removal that should bury slower midrange decks.

The call on Humans proved correct: 2 of 4 semifinalists were GW Humans, plus Naya
Humans at 8th. Abzan swept Mombo in Swiss, handled Henning's Ensoul (won R4 2-1),
and beat Max Q. (R5 2-1).

WHAT DIDN'T WORK — THE MARDU MIDRANGE MATCHUP:
The structural problem is that Abzan Control's game plan requires setup time.
Seasons Past costs 6 mana. Sedgemoor Witch needs spells. Professor of Symbology is
a 2/1 that needs a turn to settle. Against Mardu Midrange, every one of those turns
is contested:

- Duress strips Seasons Past or Vanquish before it can be cast.
- Humiliate strips a card and replaces itself via learn — tutors what beats
  your current position.
- Graveyard Trespasser actively hates out Seasons Past. It exiles graveyard
  cards on attack, denying future recursion targets.
- Sorin generates a 1/1 flier + card advantage on the same turn you're trying
  to stabilize. Flier damage doesn't interact with pest tokens on the ground.
- Florian rewards attacking into an open board after a sweeper — if you tap
  out for Vanquish, the next turn Florian draws cards through the new threats.

The result: outtempoed, put on the backfoot of the value game plan. Abzan needed to
reach a critical mass of resources (sweeper + recursion + disruption) that Mardu
prevented by stripping pieces one at a time.

POTENTIAL COUNTER-STRATEGY:
Culling Ritual (already in the sideboard) could have been the secret weapon —
against a Mardu board of 2-cost creatures and a Sorin token, Culling Ritual at BB
wipes them all and generates mana. Tainted Adversary's menace and zombie generation
applies pressure that Mardu can't cleanly answer with spot removal (zombie tokens
replenish). The tools were in the sideboard; the matchup understanding was the gap.


ARCHETYPE ANALYSIS
------------------
SELESNYA HUMANS (Fabian M 3rd / Mombo 4th)
Two meaningfully different builds. Fabian M ran mana acceleration (Elvish Mystic x3,
Avacyn's Pilgrim x4) into top-end threats (Archangel Avacyn, Tovolar's Huntmaster,
Garruk Relentless) — effectively a humans-shell ramp strategy. Mombo ran true tribal:
Champion of the Parish, Adeline, Hopeful Initiate, Thalia main deck, Torens — leaning
into go-wide pump synergy. Both had 10 Swiss points but diverged in the bracket:
Fabian beat Mombo twice. The ramp strategy had a higher power ceiling but was more
vulnerable to removal-heavy draws.

IZZET ENSOUL (Henning Lloyd Dünner, 5th — 9 points, 3W-2L)
This is the "Shrapnel Blast deck" that was anticipated — just differently framed.
Henning used Shrapnel Blast not as a standalone burn spell but as an artifact
finisher: Ornithopter + Darksteel Citadel + Ensoul Artifact creates a 5/5 flying or
5/5 indestructible on turn 2-3. Delver of Secrets, Stormchaser Drake, and Suspicious
Stowaway were the supporting threats, Fading Hope for interaction, Serpentine Ambush
as a token generator. Shrapnel Blast then did 5 damage for 2 mana by sacrificing
an artifact — a burn spell attached to an aggro plan rather than standalone burn.

Swiss losses were to Lei Gao (R5, 2-1) and Julian (R4, 2-1). Both top-2 finishers
took this deck to three games. Lei Gao's sideboard ran Fracture specifically for
Ensoul Artifact.

RAKDOS VAMPIRES (Ricardo 6th / Andy 7th / Niklas 19th)
Three completely different builds. Ricardo ran tribal midrange: Bloodthirsty Adversary,
Bloodvial Purveyor, Dominating Vampire, Henrika Domnathi (4/4 flying deathtouch that
flips into a draw engine), Slaughter Specialist. Andy ran aggressive curve: Falkenrath
Gorger, Voldaren Epicure, Cemetery Gatekeeper into Olivia Voldaren, using Blood tokens
as resources. Niklas ran the weakest build and finished last.

The archetype had the widest variance of any family: 6th, 7th, and dead last.
Ricardo's success came from a better-structured threat curve and flexible sideboard
(Graveyard Trespasser in the board, Vampires' Vengeance as a wrath). Andy's raw
aggro couldn't convert against midrange and control. Three pilots, three results.

BG VARIANTS (best finish 10th — Luc, BG Graveyard)
Five different pilots, five different angles, no top-4 representation. BG's primary
tools (pest tokens, graveyard recursion, sacrifice outlets) are incremental and
setup-dependent. Against Mardu's surgical disruption the setup gets dismantled.
Against Humans going wide on turns 2-3 the BG engines are too slow. Luc's BG
Graveyard list did best because graveyard-based redundancy is inherently resilient
to spot removal — but it still couldn't push into the bracket.

CONTROL (Teasy UW 12th / Simon Izzet 9th)
Teasy's opponent win% of 61.33% with only 6 match points tells the story: played
against extremely strong opponents (including Lei Gao R4 2-0, Henning R3 2-0),
couldn't convert games despite playing the best opponents. Simon's Izzet Control
at 9th — 7 points, solid but not finals-caliber. Izzet Control without Snapcaster
Mage (banned) lacks the recursion to truly dominate late.

The single biggest miss was not anticipating Mardu Midrange. In a format where red
cards are constrained, pivoting into a three-color midrange shell with white removal
and black disruption isn't an obvious call — but it was the exact right answer.
Mardu combined the tempo of aggressive bodies with incremental card advantage engines
and surgical hand disruption. It was the deck that could beat both aggro (efficient
bodies, Burn Down the House access) and control (Duress into threats).

SUMMARY VERDICT
---------------
The event was won by the deck no one predicted — Mardu Midrange — which answered
the field's questions while asking its own. Humans was correctly anticipated as the
volume archetype, and Abzan Control was the right counter to it. The finals matchup
(Abzan vs Mardu) was the format's two best-defined macro-strategies colliding:
inevitable control vs efficient tempo. Mardu's answer was to never let the control
player reach inevitability.

The BG variants were the tournament's collective misread — high representation, low
conversion. The absence of Meathook Massacre and Bloodtithe Harvester meant those
engines couldn't stabilize against faster tempo decks.

For next time: the ban list is the first thing to read before predicting the meta.
The absence of Lightning Strike, Fiery Temper, and Thermo-Alchemist isn't a footnote
— it's an archetype deletion.
